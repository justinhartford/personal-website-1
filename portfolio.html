My Core Principles

  1. I put the user first, always. Everything starts with: Is this actually helpful? I avoid dark patterns and extractive tactics. Trust compounds, tricks decay.
  2. I invest in communities, not funnels. Strong communities are built by being useful, generous, and human. I contribute before I convert.
  3. I make things I'd personally consume. If I wouldn't read it, click it, or find it valuable, I don't want my name on it.
  4. I favor compounding systems over one-off wins. I build loops and flywheels, not spikes that disappear.
  5. I choose long-term impact over short-term optics. I'm willing to delay gratification for lasting value.
  6. I treat brand and trust as real assets. Every interaction leaves a residue. Consistency and integrity matter.
  7. I use paid channels to learn, not to posture. Fast channels are laboratories that teach me about messaging and audiences.
  8. I value impact over activity. Motion is not progress. I focus on work that meaningfully moves things forward.
  9. I'm data-informed, not data-paralyzed. I love good data, but also trust judgment and experience. When conviction is high, I move.
  10. I never forget distribution. Creation without distribution is half-finished. Getting it in front of the right people is part of the craft.

How I Collaborate

  • I work iteratively. Small, shippable increments over long build cycles. I want feedback early to adjust before cost accumulates.
  • I share early and in public (internally). I default to transparency. I share drafts and half-formed ideas so others can shape them with me.
  • I optimize for clarity over cleverness. If something is hard to understand, I assume it's my responsibility to make it clearer.
  • I avoid silos. I loop people in early, especially those downstream. Surprises are fun at parties, not in operations.
  • I have a bias toward action. I'd rather test a reasonable idea than debate a perfect one indefinitely.
  • I seek direct contact with users and reality. Dashboards are useful, but conversations are irreplaceable.

What You Can Expect From Me

  • Thoughtful work over performative work
  • Clear communication and visible progress
  • Respect for users, teammates, and craft
  • Willingness to experiment, learn, and adapt
  • Preference for building things that last
